Transparent by Design

From Hive to Home

We believe you deserve to know exactly how your honey is made. Here is an honest look at our entire process, step by step.

1
Step 01

Careful Foraging

Our bees forage freely across pesticide-free meadows, wildflower fields, and forest edges. We carefully site our hives in locations rich with diverse floral sources, ensuring our bees have access to the widest variety of nectars throughout the season. The diversity of their forage is what gives our wildflower honey its uniquely complex, layered flavor.

2
Step 02

Ethical Harvesting

We only harvest surplus honey — never taking so much that the colony would struggle. Our beekeepers gently brush bees off the frames by hand, without harsh smoke or chemicals. Fully-capped frames are transported to our on-farm extraction room the same day to preserve maximum freshness and nutritional integrity.

3
Step 03

Cold Extraction

Frames are uncapped and spun in a stainless-steel extractor. The honey is gravity-strained through a coarse mesh to remove wax, without any heat or high-pressure filtration that would destroy delicate pollen and enzymes. We never exceed 95°F — the natural temperature of the hive — ensuring all nutrients remain fully intact.

4
Step 04

Pure Bottling

Our honey is poured directly into glass jars and sealed — that's it. No additives, no preservatives, no artificial colors or flavors. Each jar is labelled by harvest date and floral source so you always know exactly what you're getting. We use glass exclusively to protect the honey's natural flavor and prevent any chemical leaching.

Our Quality Commitments

Every jar we sell is backed by these guarantees.

Our honey is never heated above 95°F, preserving all natural enzymes, antioxidants, and vitamins.

🔬

🫙
